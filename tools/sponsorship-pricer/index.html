How to Price Your Podcast or Faceless Channel

What Is the Podcast & Avatar Sponsorship Pricer?

The Podcast & Avatar Sponsorship Pricer is a free, real-time brand deal calculator built specifically for independent creators — podcasters, faceless YouTube channels, and VTubers — who want to stop leaving money on the table when negotiating with sponsors.

Unlike flat-rate "influencer pricing" tools designed for Instagram or TikTok, this calculator is grounded in podcast and video CPM economics. It accounts for four variables that genuinely move the needle on your sponsorship rate:

  • Average views or downloads — your reach, the most fundamental input any advertiser cares about.
  • Base CPM — the market rate per thousand impressions, which you negotiate based on your niche and audience quality.
  • Niche multiplier — because a finance audience is worth dramatically more to an advertiser than a general entertainment one.
  • Ad placement type — a full dedicated episode integration is an entirely different product with a different price ceiling than a pre-roll ad.

The Industry Standard: How CPM Really Works

CPM (Cost Per Mille) means "cost per thousand impressions." If a sponsor pays a $30 CPM and your episode gets 20,000 downloads, the math is straightforward: (20,000 ÷ 1,000) × $30 = $600 per episode.

But the CPM figure itself is not fixed. It is a negotiated starting point shaped by your audience's purchasing power, your niche's advertiser demand, and your own leverage. Here is the reality of the current market:

  • Podcasts: CPMs range from $18–$50 for host-read ads; dedicated sponsorship episodes can command $50–$100+.
  • YouTube (faceless channels): CPMs vary wildly — $15–$40 for general content, $40–$80 for finance and software tutorial content.
  • VTubers & avatar channels: Consistent viewership in the 10K–100K range can realistically target $20–$45 CPM for direct deals.

The most important thing to understand: never accept a flat rate without converting it to an implied CPM. If a brand offers you $200 for a 50,000-view episode, that is a $4 CPM — a fraction of the fair market rate. Use this calculator as your sanity check.

Why Faceless Creators Can Charge Premium Rates

There is a myth that you need a face-cam or a verified blue checkmark to attract serious brand budgets. The data tells a different story.

Parasocial Trust Is the Real Asset

Faceless YouTube channels and VTubers cultivate an exceptionally deep listener-to-creator bond. When your audience cannot see your face, they project their trust onto your voice, your format, and your consistency. Studies show that host-read ads outperform display advertising by 4–6× in purchase intent.

Engagement Rate Matters More Than Subscriber Count

A faceless finance channel with 15,000 highly engaged subscribers watching 80% of each video is more valuable to a fintech advertiser than a talking-head channel with 500,000 subscribers and 3% engagement. When approaching brands, lead with your Average View Duration and Click-Through Rate. Frame your rate card around audience quality, not raw numbers.

The Niche Multiplier: Why Finance Pays More

Not all audiences are worth the same to advertisers. The CPM disparity across niches exists because of one factor: the lifetime value (LTV) of a converted customer. A listener who buys a financial product is worth hundreds or thousands of dollars to the advertiser. A gamer who buys a $2.99 mobile app is not.

  • Finance / Crypto (×1.5): High-LTV products; audience actively researches before purchasing.
  • Tech / Software (×1.3): SaaS subscriptions and hardware have high recurring revenue.
  • General / Lifestyle (×1.0): Broad appeal; D2C brands, supplements, and productivity tools.
  • Gaming / Entertainment (×0.8): High volume, lower LTV; gaming peripheral and app brands dominate.

How to Upsell Brands to a Package Deal

A single-episode sponsorship is fine. A multi-episode package is a business. Brands do not buy reach, they buy frequency. Ad recall studies consistently show that a listener needs to hear a brand message 3–7 times before they take action. A 6-episode campaign hits it reliably.

Your Upsell Script

When a brand says "we'd love to do a one-off test," respond with:

"Totally happy to start there. Based on listener behavior data, single-episode placements typically see 40–60% lower conversion compared to a 4-episode run. For the same budget spread across four episodes, you get meaningful frequency and a proper read on performance. Want me to put together a package rate?"

This works because you are reframing the spend as risk reduction, not a bigger ask.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is this calculator?

The calculator uses the industry-standard CPM formula used by podcast agencies and YouTube MCNs. The niche and placement multipliers are derived from aggregated market data. It gives you a defensible floor rate — meaning you should never accept less than what this tool outputs.

What CPM should I use for my first deal?

Start with $20–$25 as your base CPM. Once you can show a sponsor conversion data, you have the leverage to push toward $35–$50 CPM. Never start with $10 — it anchors your value at the floor.

What does the 20% agency cut represent?

The agency cut reflects the standard talent manager or creator agency commission (typically 15–25%). If you are self-managed, you keep 100% of the deal. Always negotiate your gross rate, not your net take-home.
