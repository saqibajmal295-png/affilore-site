What is the Rule of 72?

The Rule of 72 is a simplified mental math formula used in finance to estimate the number of years required to double the value of an investment at a fixed annual rate of return.

How to use it

Simply divide the number 72 by your annual return rate. For example, if you are buying an asset that yields an 8% return per year, you divide 72 by 8. The result is 9, meaning it will take approximately 9 years for your capital to double.

Asset Creation vs. Debt

The Rule of 72 is a double-edged sword. It shows how fast wealth grows, but it also shows how fast debt can crush you. If you have credit card debt at 24% interest, that debt will double in just 3 years (72 รท 24 = 3). This highlights the importance of ethical, debt-free wealth building.
