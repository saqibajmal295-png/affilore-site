Why Freelancers Undercharge (And How to Fix It)

The biggest mistake new freelancers make is calculating their rate like an employee. If you earned $50/hour at your full-time job, you might think $60/hour is a raise. It is not.

As a freelancer, you have two hidden costs that employees don't:

  • Unbillable Time: You don't get paid for the time you spend finding clients, writing proposals, or managing invoices. This tool factors that in.
  • Overhead & Taxes: You are now the employer and the employee. You pay both halves of FICA taxes, plus your own software and health insurance.
