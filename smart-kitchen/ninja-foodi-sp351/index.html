Ninja SP151 Air Fryer Toaster Oven Combo Review (2026): A Brilliant Space-Saver or a Cleaning Nightmare?

Verified Hands-On Review Updated: April 21, 2026 Extensive Cooking Tests

Sick of complex, bulky ovens taking up your entire kitchen counter? For the average American home cook, countertop real estate is arguably the most valuable space in the house. You probably want the crispy, guilt-free results of a basket air fryer and the reliable versatility of a toaster oven, but you don't want your kitchen to look like a cluttered appliance showroom.

Enter the Ninja Air Fryer Toaster Oven Combo (Model SP151). This 1800-watt, stainless steel powerhouse promises to replace multiple appliances with its 8-in-1 functionality, boasting the ability to air fry, roast, broil, bake, and toast. But its most famous party trick? When the cooking is done, it literally flips up and away to lean against your backsplash.

Does this space-saving "Swiss Army knife" of countertop ovens actually deliver restaurant-worthy meals, or is it a gimmicky gadget destined to gather dust? After analyzing extensive cooking tests, real-world customer experiences, and long-term reliability reports, here is the unbiased, authoritative truth about the Ninja SP151.

The Pros & Cons

Before we dive into the nitty-gritty details, let's look at a quick snapshot of what works beautifully and what desperately needs improvement.

✓ The Pros

  • Flip-Away Storage: Reclaims up to 50% of your counter space by flipping vertically against your backsplash when not in use.
  • The 60-Second Preheat: While your traditional wall oven takes 10 to 15 minutes to warm up, the Ninja SP151 is ready to cook in roughly 60 seconds.
  • Exceptional Toasting: Unlike many air fryers, this appliance excels at basic toasting, accommodating up to 6 slices of bread with highly even browning.
  • Wide Cooking Area: The spacious, flat basket allows you to spread food out in a single layer, which yields vastly superior crispiness compared to deep, crowded basket fryers.
  • Energy Efficient: Uses up to 80% less energy than firing up a traditional full-size electric wall oven.

✗ The Cons

  • A Nightmare to Clean: The incredibly short interior height means grease splatters directly onto the upper heating elements, baking on instantly and making deep cleaning a massive chore.
  • Vertical Limitations: You absolutely cannot roast a whole chicken or fit standard, deep baking pans inside this oven. It is strictly for "flat" foods.
  • Awkward Handle Placement: The door handle is located exclusively on the left side, which right-handed users often find ergonomically frustrating.
  • Longevity Concerns: The touch control panel and wiring are prone to heat and moisture fatigue, with a noticeable pattern of catastrophic failures occurring right around the 13-to-18-month mark.

Deep Dive: The 3 Most Unique Features

The Ninja SP151 separates itself from the sea of cheap, plastic basket fryers and clunky toaster ovens with three highly innovative features.

1. "Flip Up & Away" Storage Capability

The most eye-catching aspect of this appliance is its transformational footprint. When laying flat for cooking, the oven takes up a hefty chunk of space, measuring roughly 17 inches deep and almost 16 inches wide. However, the magic happens when you are done cooking.

Once the unit cools down—the digital display will safely let you know it's ready—you can grab the handle and rotate the entire oven 90 degrees upward. In this vertical position, it tucks neatly against your kitchen backsplash. For urban dwellers, apartment renters, or anyone with limited prep space, this design reclaims 50% of your counter area.

The Catch: You must remember to empty the removable crumb tray before flipping it. If you don't, loose food particles, breadcrumbs, and grease will migrate into the back of the housing, which can create smoke and nasty smells the next time you turn it on.

2. OvenCrisp Technology & 8-in-1 Versatility

Standard basket air fryers typically just blast hot air. The Ninja SP151 steps it up with what Ninja calls "OvenCrisp Technology," which combines infrared heating elements with rapid, circulating cyclonic air to deliver edge-to-edge even cooking.

This 1800-watt machine is a true 8-in-1 multi-tasker. Using the digital controls, you can select between Air Fry, Air Roast, Air Broil, Bake, Pizza, Toast, Bagel, and Dehydrate. Because the hot air circulates at different speeds depending on the function, it cooks up to 65% faster than a traditional electric oven. Furthermore, cooking at these high temperatures yields up to 75% less fat than traditional deep-frying methods (tested against hand-cut, deep-fried French fries).

3. Extra-Large Capacity, Small Vertical Footprint

If you have ever used a standard pod-shaped air fryer, you know the frustration of having to stack your food. Stacking leads to steaming, which ruins the crunch. The Ninja SP151 solves this by trading vertical height for horizontal width.

The included 13x13-inch nonstick sheet pan and wire air fry basket allow you to lay all your food out flat. You can easily fit a 12-inch frozen pizza, toast 6 slices of bread uniformly, or cook 6 standard chicken breasts at once. By keeping ingredients like French fries, shrimp, and chicken nuggets in a single, uncrowded layer, the hot air hits maximum surface area, resulting in restaurant-quality crunch without having to constantly open the oven to shake a basket.

Cooking Performance: Does It Actually Work?

When it comes to everyday cooking, the SP151 is an absolute workhorse that excels in a few specific areas.

  • Air Frying: The wide, shallow basket is the golden rule for perfect air frying. French fries, tater tots, and breaded chicken wings come out shatteringly crisp and evenly browned.
  • Toasting: Many air fryer-toaster oven combos fail miserably at basic toasting. This unit is an exception. It accommodates multiple slices of bread or bagel halves and browns them with incredible uniformity, whether you want a light golden hue or a dark crunch.
  • Baking & Roasting: You can easily bake a batch of cookies or a shallow pan of brownies. It is also fantastic for quick, 20-minute "sheet-pan" dinners featuring a flat protein (like salmon or chicken) and roasted vegetables. However, because the heating elements are so close to the food, you will often need to dial down the temperature or cooking time compared to box instructions to prevent burning the edges.

The Elephant in the Room: Ergonomics, Cleaning, & Longevity

If there are fatal flaws in the Ninja SP151, they revolve around the daily user experience and the machine's long-term durability.

The Left-Sided Handle Flaw:
In a bizarre design choice, Ninja placed the door handle exclusively on the left side of the oven door. For right-handed users, this is a constant ergonomic annoyance. You often hold your plate or tongs in your dominant right hand, forcing you to reach across your body awkwardly with your right hand—or fumble with your left hand—to open the hot oven door.

A Nightmare to Clean:
Because the interior cavity is incredibly short, your food sits dangerously close to the upper heating elements and the ceiling. When you air fry fatty foods like chicken wings or burgers, the grease aerosolizes and splatters directly onto the metal interior. Thanks to the intense heat, this grease carbonizes and bakes onto the surface almost instantly. Standard dish soap and a soft sponge simply won't cut it.

While the bottom of the oven does drop open to give you better access, owners have resorted to extreme cleaning hacks to cut through the baked-on grime, with many relying heavily on soaking the interior in Dawn Powerwash. If you neglect cleaning it, the oven will begin to smoke heavily and fill your house with the smell of burnt fat.

The "Month 13" Longevity Curse:
We must also address the reliability of the Ninja SP151. A disturbing number of users report that right after the 1-year warranty expires, the unit faces catastrophic electrical errors.

Because the oven uses a capacitive touch panel rather than physical analog dials, it is highly sensitive to moisture. Every time you cook, heat and moisture from your food create condensation inside the unit. Over 12 to 18 months, this moisture migrates behind the front panel, eventually causing the control board to short out or die completely, rendering the machine a useless paperweight. Additionally, the repetitive mechanical stress of flipping the oven up and down can fatigue the internal wiring over time.

The Final Verdict: Who Is This For?

The Ninja SP151 Air Fryer Toaster Oven is a masterclass in kitchen compromise. It was beautifully designed to answer a very specific question: How can we cook meals faster, with less fat, without permanently eating up our counter space?

This product is BEST for:

  • Urban Dwellers & Small Kitchen Owners: If your counter space is limited, the ability to flip this oven up and reclaim your prep area is an unbeatable, game-changing luxury.
  • The "Sheet-Pan" Chef: If your diet consists of roasted vegetables, flat proteins (steak, salmon, chicken breasts), and pizzas, the wide basket will give you fast, restaurant-quality results.
  • Toast Lovers: If you want an appliance that genuinely replaces your pop-up toaster without burning your bagels, this is one of the best on the market.

You should SKIP this product if:

  • You love whole roasts: The low ceiling means you cannot fit a whole rotisserie chicken or bake a tall loaf of bread.
  • You hate deep cleaning: If the thought of scrubbing baked-on grease off the ceiling of a tiny oven sounds like torture, you will quickly grow to resent this appliance.
  • You want a "Buy It For Life" appliance: Given the well-documented control board failures around the 18-month mark, this is an appliance you are likely renting for a couple of years rather than investing in for a decade.

Ultimately, the Ninja SP151 is a high-performance, incredibly versatile machine that will absolutely speed up your daily cooking and save you precious space. As long as you are willing to keep up with the maintenance, accept the vertical limits, and view it as a medium-term investment, it is one of the smartest upgrades you can make to your daily cooking routine.
