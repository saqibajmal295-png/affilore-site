The Brutal Truth: Flaws, Constraints, and What You Need to Know

At Affilore, we believe in being entirely honest with our readers. While the MEATER Plus is a fantastic piece of technology, it is not flawless. Before you spend your money, you need to understand exactly what this device can and cannot do. Here are the three most critical "brutal truths" you need to know about the MEATER Plus.

1. The Signal Range Reality (The "Faraday Cage" Effect)

Let's talk about the meater plus bluetooth range. The marketing materials advertise an obstructed range of up to 165 feet. However, the reality of cooking in thick-walled US smokers—like a heavy-duty Traeger or a ceramic Big Green Egg—tells a different story.

The moment you close the lid on a heavy smoker, the thick metal or ceramic walls act as a "Faraday cage," blocking the Bluetooth 4.0 signal from escaping. Many 1-star reviews come from users who walk inside their house and instantly lose connection.

The Fix: It is not magic; it is Bluetooth. To keep a stable connection, you must keep the magnetic wooden charging block right next to your grill. The block acts as a Bluetooth repeater, catching the weak signal from inside the grill and broadcasting it to your phone. As a pro-tip, keep the wooden block touching the grill lid or within 5 feet of the cooker, and your connection dropouts will disappear.

2. The Ambient Temp Warning: Do Not Sear!

The MEATER Plus features two sensors: an internal sensor located at the tip of the probe, and an ambient (pit) sensor located in the black ceramic handle. The internal sensor can withstand up to 212°F (100°C), which is plenty for the inside of any meat.

However, the ambient sensor is strictly rated for a maximum of 527°F (275°C). This is a hard limit. Unlike newer models, this original version cannot handle direct flame searing. If you try to use the MEATER Plus over a high-heat open charcoal flame or during a blazing hot reverse-sear, the black ceramic end of the probe will crack, melt, or completely fry the internal electronics.

The Fix: Keep this thermometer strictly for roasting, smoking, baking, and indirect grilling. If you want to sear a steak, remove the probe first.

3. The AAA Battery Fix

The MEATER Plus probe charges itself while sitting inside its stylish bamboo wooden block. The block itself is powered by a single AAA battery. A massive portion of user complaints regarding a "dead probe" or a device that "won't charge" all point back to this simple feature.

The Fix: If your probe isn't charging or won't turn on, 90% of the time you do not have a broken device—you just need to replace the AAA battery located behind the magnetic back cover of the wooden dock. Once you swap that single battery, the base will easily provide up to 100 probe charges.

Original MEATER vs MEATER 2: What's the Difference?

If you are currently debating the original meater vs meater 2 (now officially rebranded as the MEATER Pro), it comes down to a few distinct technological leaps.

The original MEATER Plus features a 6mm thick stainless steel probe with a single internal sensor and one ambient sensor. Because it only has one internal sensor at the tip, accuracy relies heavily on you getting the tip into the exact dead-center, coldest part of the meat.

The newer MEATER 2 Plus (Pro), on the other hand, is a completely redesigned device. Here is how the Pro beats the original:

  • Thinner Design: The Pro is 5mm thick, representing a 30% reduction in cross-sectional area, meaning it leaves a much smaller hole in your food.
  • Extreme Heat Resistance: It features an all-metal build with a tiny zirconia ceramic band, allowing it to withstand blistering ambient temperatures up to 932°F (500°C). You can finally use it over an open flame or in a deep fryer.
  • Six Total Sensors: The Pro packs 5 internal sensors and 1 ambient sensor. The app utilizes all five internal sensors to automatically locate the true "coldest point" of the meat, making your cook far more accurate.
  • Faster Charging: A 30-minute charge on the Pro yields 24 hours of cooking time, while the original takes much longer to juice up.

However, the Pro usually retails around $129 to $139. If you are mostly doing low-and-slow smoking or oven roasting, those premium features might be overkill for your needs.

The Foolproof MEATER App

Hardware is only half the battle; the software is where MEATER truly shines. The MEATER app is universally praised for its "Guided Cook" system, which is practically foolproof for beginners.

When you set up a cook, you simply select your meat type (beef, pork, poultry, fish, etc.), the specific cut, and your desired target temperature (like medium-rare). The app monitors the ambient temperature of your grill alongside the internal temperature of the meat to calculate highly accurate estimates of when your food will be done.

Even better, the app accounts for "carry-over cooking"—the fact that meat continues to rise in temperature after you take it off the heat. The app will actively notify you to remove the meat *before* it hits your target temperature, ensuring that your medium-rare steak doesn't turn into a well-done hockey puck while it rests on the cutting board.
